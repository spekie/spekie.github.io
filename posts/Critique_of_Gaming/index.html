Critique of Gaming

Hello there. By the title of this post, you can tell that I will criticise gaming because I have stopped gaming recently, just to take a bit of a break from it. This post is broken up into sections with separate headers, each describing my reasons for this, as well as my critique of gaming, to keep the post organized and consistent. Something to note is that this is written from a PC gaming perspective and Linux user perspective, which shows my bias just for you to be aware, and to also consider that this only covers that particular perspective.

Time

I will start with one of the important factors of life, which is time. I dedicate most of my time to other things other than gaming, while still sometimes on the computer, like coding, which is what I mostly do. I have everything planned out, and I do other things as well, classes and what not have been a bother, and many other things. I simply do not have time anymore to continue, and I just never noticed or cared to game anymore.

Corporations

The absolutely disgusting behaviour of corporations in releasing new games with various issues really made me question the gaming industry as a whole. I will go further to elaborate that the games do have issues, both compatibility-wise and content-wise. Compatibility-wise, a lot of games require an extremely expensive and powerful computer to be played at a decent and playable framerate; a lot of games do not even work on other operating systems by being held back through invasive anti-cheat software, and other incompatibilities, or just being developed absolutely poorly. Content-wise, the gaming industry suffers from corporate greed, with games that are considered "Triple-A" now being the standard price of 70 dollars, content being unfinished, and being released sooner just to get the game out into the market as quickly as possible in order to achieve some sort of profit when there is a sale of some kind. I am aware that not all corporations or companies are like this, and not all games are terrible that release today, but sometimes it does feel like there are way more games created by these companies that are just meant to get a little bit of profit and please their stockholders. There are a lot of good independent games that are available on Steam and even have native support for Linux, which brings us to the next point.

Valve Corporation and Steam

  • The famous company that apparently cares about Linux despite Linux gaming being completely dead before their product, known as the "Steam Deck," released with the Proton compatibility layer, which is a fork of the Wine project tailored for gaming and compatibility with Steam, a product of Valve, which is a store for games.
  • Valve has explicitly mentioned in their agreement that you do not own any games; you are simply buying a license or any other similar term to that in order to play the game. Some people might not see an issue here because that is fairly common, especially in various services these days.
  • Another criticism of Valve is their favouring of more known publishers or game developers that are large corporations with their percentage cut on revenue, which leaves barely anything for small developers of independent games.
  • The use of DRM in the Steam client is also something I do not like which is also a famous critique, and the Steam client is also proprietary software, the only piece of proprietary software I had installed other than the various proprietary or binary blobs that come with the Linux kernel, which I do not personally mind as they are needed for hardware compatibility.
  • In all due respect, I appreciate Valve and their efforts in Linux gaming, but I think personally gaming on Linux is being held by string and duct tape right now.

Conclusion

In summary, I just stopped gaming that is it. I do not see it as valuable to me anymore, not even for recreation. My critique of gaming and the industry is explained in brief summary here, I still have a lot to say, and this could be as long as a 5 hour college lecture. One more thing about gaming, that is simply a reality, is hardware compatibility which tends to be greater on console since games are tailored for consoles and all computers can be different which is where gaming on personal computers really has a disadvantage.
