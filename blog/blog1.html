New PC

First piece of my actual writing on this website. I have finally built another computer throughout my time, which I personally use. It is nothing too impressive, but as long as it works for me, it is great. Link to PC build on PCPartPicker here.

There are a few reasons as to why I chose these components in particular; some people might just think I am copying Linus Torvalds' PC build from his interview with ZDNET. But I mostly care about a system that can last for a long time, which is why the PSU is SeaSonic and gold certified. I might later switch to a platinum-certified PSU because I have a few power supplies laying around to sell and buy another one. The case is chosen wisely because of the CPU cooler, the NH-D15, which might not fit in some cases but for be quiet's cases it will definitely fit due to be quiet also making excellent coolers, which for me are not on par with Noctua's. The GPU is not important to me; I do not play many games; however, the RX 580 was chosen because of its value and 8 gigabytes of VRAM. For RAM, 32 gigabytes is enough for most people, including me. While DDR5 is relatively new, it seems promising and also faster. The motherboard is just a simple Gigabyte motherboard because they work the best and do not have extra features that I do not use that are only exclusive to Windows because I do not use Windows. Storage is not an issue at all; I have multiple SSD's. I try not to use HDDs that much but still have to due to the availability of them.

The peripherals are quite old except for the mouse, which is obviously Logitech; I have not used anything else other than that. The keyboard is quite ancient and has some modifications that I made to it myself so it would be more pleasing to type on it.

My choice of software, I won't really talk about here that much. I use Arch Linux for my Linux distribution of choice because of its rolling release model, the AUR, which has everything you need. People can get the same benefits of the AUR if the software they need is on Flatpak's; however, the AUR still has a lot of packages second to NixOS' unofficial packages. People should only look at a few things when using a Linux distribution, which is mainly the package manager and init system. The desktop environments and all kinds of other things that a distribution configures can be done by yourself. People who do not want to do that can just use the already configured version of that distribution; a prime example of this is Debian. Some people find it difficult to configure or even install, so most people are using Ubuntu, which is practically a fork of Debian but with a configured desktop environment and many more additional features. Desktop environments can be switched; I personally use KDE Plasma due to more freedom from GNOME's minimal design and actual official customization rather than installing third-party extensions that may or may not work.

To conclude on a simple note, good computer. Also read the news some day and maybe switch to Linux if you can, because proprietary operating systems like Microsofts' Windows are adding unnecessary features, spyware, and just more garbage. Linux grew in market share recently, so maybe take at least one step and boot it from a USB flash drive. Thank you for reading.
